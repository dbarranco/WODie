# WODs — Lower Body

## WOD-LB-001 — Lower Body Power Development
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- box

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Standing hip mobility - circles in both directions*
- Pigeon Pose — 60 seconds each side — *Deep hip external rotation stretch*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *90/90 lunge position, stretch front of hip*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Bodyweight squat, full depth*
- Inchworm — 8 reps — *Walk hands out to plank, walk feet to hands*
- Box Jump (low height) — 8 reps — *Submaximal height, focus on landing mechanics*
- Good Morning — 10 reps — *Empty barbell, hinge pattern activation*

### 💪 Back Squat - Ramp to Heavy Double (22 min)

- **Back Squat** — 5×5, 4, 3, 2, 2
  > *Ramp progression - progressively heavier loads with decreasing reps. Final two sets are working sets at 83-85%.*
  > **Scale:** Goblet squat or dumbbell squat

### 🔥 Metcon — 12 minutes (15 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 10 Box Jump *(Scale: Step-up or reduce height)*
- 15 Kettlebell Swing (Russian) *(Scale: Reduce weight)*
- 20 Air Squat *(Scale: Reduce reps to maintain form)*

**Target:** 7-10 rounds

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (8 min)

- Foam Roll - Quads — 90 seconds each leg — *Slow passes, pause on tender spots*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Deeper hold than warmup, relax into stretch*
- Glute Bridge — 2x15 reps — *Bodyweight, hold contraction for 2 seconds at top*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** This session targets lower body power development through the combination of heavy back squat work (ATP-PC system, neural adaptation) followed by a glycolytic metcon emphasizing explosive lower body movements. The squat-first sequencing ensures CNS freshness for maximal force production. The 12-minute AMRAP keeps the time domain in the glycolytic zone (10 sec - 2 min high-intensity efforts) without excessive oxidative demand that would interfere with strength adaptation. *— Wilson et al 2012 - Concurrent Training Meta-Analysis*

**Movement selection:** Back squat is the primary lower body strength movement, developing force production in the squat pattern. Box jumps develop rate of force development (power), kettlebell swings train the hinge pattern with explosive hip extension, and air squats provide volume under fatigue. This combination addresses three of Glassman's ten physical skills: strength, power, and stamina. *— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading rationale:** The ramp/build-up progression scheme (5@65%, 4@72%, 3@78%, 2@83%, 2@85%) is appropriate for Week 1 baseline establishment and neural preparation. This progressive loading prepares the nervous system for heavier loads while allowing technique refinement under progressively challenging weights. Zatsiorsky & Kraemer describe progressive warm-up sets as essential for neural preparation before maximal efforts. The 180-second rest intervals allow phosphocreatine replenishment for strength work above 80% 1RM. *— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training*

## WOD-LB-002 — Hinge Pattern Strength
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rowing machine

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Cat-Cow — 15 reps — *Spinal flexion/extension mobility drill*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *90/90 position hip opener*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Lunge with rotation, comprehensive lower body mobility*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Good Morning — 12 reps — *Empty barbell, hinge pattern primer*
- Glute Bridge — 15 reps — *Posterior chain activation*
- Romanian Deadlift (light) — 10 reps — *45 lbs or empty bar, movement prep*
- Inchworm — 8 reps — *Hamstring and shoulder mobility*

### 💪 Deadlift - EMOM Strength (24 min)

- **Deadlift** — 8×3 reps
  > *EMOM format: Perform 3 deadlifts at the start of every 2 minutes. Faster execution earns more rest. Focus on consistent technique across all 8 rounds. Total time: 16 minutes of strength work.*
  > **Scale:** Trap bar deadlift or reduce load

### 🔥 Metcon — 5 Rounds For Time (14 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 12 Romanian Deadlift *(Scale: Dumbbell RDL or reduce weight)*
- 250/200 meters Row *(Scale: Reduce distance to 200/150m)*

**Target:** 9-12 minutes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*
- Foam Roll - Quads — 60 seconds each leg — *Post-hinge work recovery*
- Cat-Cow — 20 reps slow — *Spinal decompression after loaded hinge work*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** This session emphasizes the hinge pattern through deadlift strength work and Romanian deadlift volume in the metcon. The E2MOM strength format maintains session flow while providing adequate phosphocreatine recovery between sets. The For Time metcon stays in the medium time domain (7-15 min), targeting the glycolytic system without excessive aerobic volume that would interfere with strength adaptation. Rowing provides monostructural cardio to balance the hinge-dominant movements. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement selection:** The deadlift is the foundational hinge pattern, developing posterior chain strength and total body force production. Romanian deadlift in the metcon provides eccentric loading and hamstring volume while reinforcing the hinge pattern under fatigue. This addresses movement balance requirements for weekly hinge pattern work. The rowing machine provides monostructural cardio while complementing the posterior chain emphasis through the pulling motion. *— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading rationale:** The E2MOM format at 75% 1RM with 3 reps per round is appropriate for Week 2 building intensity while managing fatigue. The 2-minute interval allows sufficient phosphocreatine recovery for loads in the 70-85% range. This format creates auto-regulated rest - faster execution earns more recovery time, rewarding efficiency and maintaining engagement with the timer. Total volume of 8 sets x 3 reps = 24 reps falls within the 4-12 set range for hinge pattern weekly volume. *— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-LB-003 — Front Squat Volume Builder
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- assault bike

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction, each arm — *Controlled articular rotations for shoulder mobility - important for front rack position*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Seated or quadruped, improve thoracic extension for upright torso*
- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Hip joint mobility prep*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Focus on upright torso position*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Dynamic hip and thoracic mobility*
- Front Squat (empty bar) — 10 reps — *Front rack position practice, movement prep*
- Inchworm — 6 reps — *Full body warmup*

### 💪 Front Squat - Straight Sets (22 min)

- **Front Squat** — 5×5 reps
  > *Straight sets - same weight across all 5 sets. Focus on consistent depth and upright torso position. This is baseline strength development with moderate intensity and moderate volume.*
  > **Scale:** Goblet squat or dumbbell front squat

### 🔥 Metcon — 15 minutes (5 rounds) (15 min)

**Format:** EMOM | **Time cap:** 15 min

- 12 Goblet Squat *(Scale: Reduce weight or reps)*
- 12/10 calories Assault Bike *(Scale: Reduce calories to 10/8)*
- 10 Burpee *(Scale: Step-down burpee or reduce reps)*

**Target:** Complete all work with 10-20 seconds rest each minute

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (8 min)

- Foam Roll - Quads — 90 seconds each leg — *Focus on quad recovery after squat volume*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip flexor and glute release*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 slow reps each direction — *Shoulder mobility restoration after front rack work*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** This session builds squat pattern volume through front squats, which emphasize quadriceps and require greater core stability than back squats due to the anterior load position. The straight-set format at moderate intensity (72%) allows accumulation of quality volume for hypertrophy and work capacity. The 3-station EMOM metcon provides a glycolytic stimulus with lower body emphasis (goblet squats) while incorporating monostructural cardio and full-body conditioning. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement selection:** Front squat develops the squat pattern with greater emphasis on quadriceps and anterior core than back squat, providing movement variation within the squat pattern category. Goblet squats in the metcon reinforce the squat pattern under fatigue at lower loading. Assault bike provides monostructural cardio (required weekly), and burpees contribute to general physical preparedness through full-body, high-intensity movement. *— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading rationale:** Straight sets at 72% 1RM for 5x5 is classic hypertrophy and baseline strength programming, appropriate for Week 1-2 phases. This scheme is simple to execute, builds work capacity at a specific intensity, and has lower cognitive load than complex progression schemes. The 120-second rest intervals are appropriate for the hypertrophy zone (60-90 seconds is standard, but 120 seconds accommodates the compound nature of front squats). Total volume of 25 reps at 72% provides significant structural adaptation stimulus. *— Schoenfeld - Science and Development of Muscle Hypertrophy*

## WOD-LB-004 — Olympic Lift + Lower Power
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- box
- jump rope

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction, each arm — *Shoulder mobility for overhead receiving position*
- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Hip joint prep for explosive triple extension*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Thoracic mobility for overhead stability*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Good Morning — 10 reps — *Empty barbell, hinge activation*
- Hang Power Clean (light) — 8 reps — *Empty bar or 45 lbs, technique practice*
- Barbell Pass-Through — 10 reps — *Empty barbell, shoulder mobility drill*
- Box Jump (submaximal) — 6 reps — *Low height, explosive hip extension practice*

### 💪 Hang Power Clean - Wave Loading (24 min)

- **Hang Power Clean** — 6×3, 3, 3, 3, 3, 3
  > *Wave loading - oscillate between moderate and heavier loads to exploit post-activation potentiation. The heavier sets prime the nervous system, making the return to moderate weight feel lighter. Focus on explosive hip extension and fast elbows on each rep. Total time: ~18 minutes including rest.*
  > **Scale:** Dumbbell hang clean or reduce load

### 🔥 Metcon — 10 minutes (14 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 10 min

- 5 Power Clean *(Scale: Reduce to 95/65 lbs or dumbbell power clean)*
- 8 Box Jump *(Scale: Step-up or reduce height)*
- 30 Double-Under *(Scale: Single-unders x90 or reduce reps)*

**Target:** 5-7 rounds

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Foam Roll - Quads — 60 seconds each leg — *Recovery from Olympic lifting and jumping volume*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip external rotation and glute release*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 slow reps each direction — *Shoulder recovery after overhead work*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** This session targets lower body power through Olympic lifting (hang power clean) and plyometrics (box jumps). The wave loading in the strength block exploits post-activation potentiation to increase neural output. The 10-minute AMRAP stays in the short-to-medium time domain, emphasizing the glycolytic system while maintaining power output quality. Olympic lifting is placed on a fresh CNS day, not following heavy squats or deadlifts, per recovery sequencing rules. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement selection:** Hang power clean is an Olympic lift variation emphasizing explosive hip extension and rate of force development - addressing the 'power' physical skill. Box jumps develop lower body power through plyometric training. Power cleans in the metcon reinforce the movement pattern under fatigue at moderate loading. Double-unders provide coordination and ankle/calf power endurance. This combination develops power, coordination, and cardiovascular endurance per Glassman's ten physical skills. *— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading rationale:** Wave loading (3@72%, 3@77%, 3@82%, 3@72%, 3@78%, 3@83%) is appropriate for Week 2-3 strength emphasis with experienced athletes. Robbins (2005) documents post-activation potentiation effects lasting 3-10 minutes after heavy loading - the heavier sets prime the nervous system for enhanced performance on subsequent moderate-load sets. This scheme allows higher total volume at elevated intensities while managing fatigue through the oscillating pattern. The 180-second rest ensures phosphocreatine recovery for power work. *— Robbins 2005 - Post-Activation Potentiation*

## WOD-LB-005 — Squat Pattern Stamina
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- kettlebell
- ski erg

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *90/90 position, open front of hip*
- Pigeon Pose — 60 seconds each side — *Deep hip external rotation stretch*
- Cat-Cow — 15 reps — *Spinal mobility for squat positioning*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 20 reps — *Full depth, quality movement prep*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Dynamic hip and thoracic mobility*
- Goblet Squat (light) — 12 reps — *Light KB, movement pattern activation*
- Inchworm — 8 reps — *Full body warmup*

### 💪 Back Squat - Top Set + Back-Off Volume (20 min)

- **Back Squat** — 5×2, 5, 5, 5, 5
  > *Top set + back-off volume scheme. First set is heavy top set of 2 reps at 85% for neural stimulation. Then drop to 68% for 4 sets of 5 reps to accumulate hypertrophy volume. This combines peak intensity exposure with structural adaptation work in one efficient session.*
  > **Scale:** Goblet squat or dumbbell squat

### 🔥 Metcon — 4 Rounds For Time (18 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 15 min

- 20 Goblet Squat *(Scale: Reduce weight or reps)*
- 300/250 meters Ski Erg *(Scale: Reduce to 250/200m)*
- 20 Kettlebell Swing (Russian) *(Scale: Reduce weight)*

**Target:** 11-15 minutes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Foam Roll - Quads — 90 seconds each leg — *Recovery from high-rep squat volume*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Deep hip release*
- Glute Bridge — 15 reps slow — *Gentle glute activation and hip extension*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** This session develops squat pattern stamina through the combination of strength work and high-rep metcon squatting. The top set + back-off volume scheme efficiently combines neural (heavy top set) and structural (volume work) adaptations. The medium time domain metcon (11-15 min target) emphasizes the glycolytic-to-oxidative transition zone, building muscular endurance in the squat pattern while maintaining intensity below the aerobic threshold that would excessively interfere with strength gains. *— Wilson et al 2012 - Concurrent Training Meta-Analysis*

**Movement selection:** Back squat is the primary lower body strength movement. Goblet squats in the metcon provide high-rep squat pattern work under fatigue, developing the 'stamina' physical skill - the ability to process, deliver, store and use energy in repeated efforts. Ski erg provides monostructural cardio (required weekly) and complements the lower body emphasis. Kettlebell swings add hinge pattern work to balance the squat-dominant session. *— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading rationale:** The top set + back-off volume scheme is appropriate for Week 3 peak intensity sessions with intermediate athletes. The heavy top set (2@85%) provides neural stimulation and peak intensity exposure while the athlete is fresh. Back-off sets at 68% (4x5) accumulate 20 reps of quality volume for hypertrophy without excessive CNS fatigue. Bompa & Haff describe this concurrent approach as maximizing adaptation diversity within a single session - neural work followed by structural work minimizes interference while addressing multiple training goals efficiently. *— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-LB-006 — Unilateral Lower Body Focus
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- dumbbells
- kettlebell
- box
- rowing machine

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Single leg balance and hip mobility*
- Pigeon Pose — 60 seconds each side — *Hip external rotation prep*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *Lunge position hip opener*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Bilateral squat pattern warmup*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Dynamic lunge with rotation*
- Glute Bridge (single leg) — 8 each leg — *Unilateral glute activation*
- Inchworm — 8 reps — *Full body prep*

### 💪 Dumbbell Work - Single Leg Focus (22 min)

- **Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift** — 4×8 each leg
  > *Unilateral hinge pattern. Hold dumbbell in opposite hand from working leg. Focus on balance and eccentric control. Addresses asymmetries and develops single-leg stability.*
  > **Scale:** Use lighter weight or hold support for balance
- **Bulgarian Split Squat** — 4×10 each leg
  > *Rear foot elevated on box or bench. Unilateral squat pattern - builds single-leg strength and addresses imbalances. Front leg does majority of work.*
  > **Scale:** Bodyweight or reduce elevation height

### 🔥 Metcon — 14 minutes (16 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 14 min

- 20 total (10 each leg) Box Step-Up (alternating) *(Scale: Reduce height or remove weight)*
- 10 each arm Kettlebell Swing (single arm) *(Scale: Two-hand swing or lighter weight)*
- 250/200 meters Row *(Scale: Reduce to 200/150m)*

**Target:** 5-7 rounds

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Deep hip release after unilateral work*
- Foam Roll - Quads — 60 seconds each leg — *Individual leg attention post-unilateral volume*
- Glute Bridge — 15 reps slow — *Bilateral glute activation and recovery*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** This session emphasizes unilateral (single-leg) lower body work to address asymmetries and develop balance and stability - two of Glassman's ten physical skills. Unilateral training reduces load on the spine while maintaining high muscular demand, provides variety from bilateral movements, and identifies strength imbalances between limbs. The 14-minute AMRAP stays in the medium time domain, targeting the glycolytic system while accumulating unilateral movement volume. *— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Movement selection:** Single-leg Romanian deadlift develops unilateral hinge pattern strength and balance. Bulgarian split squats target the squat pattern unilaterally with significant quad and glute demand. Box step-ups in the metcon provide explosive unilateral work. Single-arm KB swings add anti-rotation core demand while maintaining the hinge pattern. Rowing provides monostructural cardio (weekly requirement) and allows recovery between unilateral efforts. This session addresses balance, coordination, and strength skills concurrently. *— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading rationale:** RPE 7-8 prescription is appropriate when training 1RM is unknown for accessory/unilateral movements. Unilateral work typically uses 50-70% of the load used for bilateral movements due to balance and stability demands. The 90-second rest between sets falls in the hypertrophy rest range, appropriate for the 8-10 rep schemes used. Four sets per movement provides 8 total working sets per leg across both exercises, which falls within weekly volume guidelines for lower body patterns while accounting for the unilateral nature reducing systemic fatigue compared to heavy bilateral work. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

## WOD-LB-007 — Posterior Chain Power + Volume
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- kettlebell
- assault bike

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Cat-Cow — 15 reps — *Spinal mobility for hinge pattern*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *Hip extension range prep*
- Pigeon Pose — 60 seconds each side — *Hip external rotation and glute prep*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Glute Bridge — 15 reps — *Posterior chain activation*
- Good Morning — 12 reps — *Empty barbell, hinge pattern prep*
- Kettlebell Swing (light) — 15 reps — *Light KB, explosive hip extension practice*
- Inchworm — 8 reps — *Hamstring and full body warmup*

### 💪 Sumo Deadlift High Pull - EMOM Power (24 min)

- **Sumo Deadlift High Pull** — 10×5 reps
  > *Every 90 seconds for 10 rounds. SDHP emphasizes explosive hip extension and vertical pull - power development for posterior chain. Moderate load allows quality speed of movement. Total time: 15 minutes of work.*
  > **Scale:** Kettlebell SDHP or reduce load

### 🔥 Metcon — 21-15-9 For Time (14 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 21-15-9 Kettlebell Swing (American) *(Scale: Russian swing (eye level) or reduce weight)*
- 15/12 - 12/9 - 9/6 calories Assault Bike *(Scale: Reduce calories by 3 each round)*

**Target:** 7-10 minutes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Deep hip and glute release after posterior chain volume*
- Cat-Cow — 20 reps slow — *Spinal decompression after hinge work*
- Glute Bridge — 15 reps slow with pause — *Gentle glute activation and hip extension*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** This session targets posterior chain power and endurance through explosive hip extension movements. SDHP in the strength block develops rate of force development in the hinge pattern at moderate loads with high velocity. The short metcon (7-10 min target) stays in the glycolytic time domain, emphasizing power endurance with American KB swings (full hip extension to overhead). The descending rep scheme (21-15-9) maintains intensity as fatigue accumulates. *— Gastin 2001 - Energy System Interaction*

**Movement selection:** Sumo deadlift high pull is a ballistic hinge movement emphasizing explosive hip extension and vertical pulling - developing power and speed (two of the ten physical skills). American kettlebell swings reinforce explosive hip extension with overhead stability demand. Assault bike provides monostructural cardio (weekly requirement) and complements the hip-dominant movements. This session addresses power, speed, and cardiovascular endurance concurrently. *— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading rationale:** The E90s (every 90 seconds) EMOM format for 10 rounds with 5 reps of SDHP is appropriate for power development. The interval is short enough to maintain work density but provides adequate recovery between explosive sets. Load at 65-70% of deadlift 1RM or RPE 7 allows maximal velocity of movement - critical for power development. Zatsiorsky & Kraemer identify that power training requires moderate loads (30-70%) moved at maximal speed. The 90-second interval allows phosphocreatine recovery between power efforts. Total volume of 50 reps across 10 sets provides significant posterior chain stimulation without excessive loading that would compromise speed. *— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training*
