# WOD Pool

## WOD-STR-001 — Back Squat Foundation + Glycolytic Push
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** strength

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- pull-up bar

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Open hip capsule, increase synovial fluid*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Hip flexor, thoracic rotation, hamstring mobility*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal segmentation and warmup*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Movement-specific warmup for squat pattern*
- Good Morning — 10 reps — *Posterior chain activation, hinge pattern prep*
- Inchworm — 8 reps — *Hamstring lengthening, shoulder stabilization*

### 💪 Back Squat — Baseline Volume (22 min)

- **Back Squat** — 5×5 @ 70% 1RM | 180 seconds between sets
  > *Straight sets. Focus on depth and bar path. Week 1 baseline loading.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 5 Rounds For Time (15 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 15 Push-Up *(Scale: Knee push-up or banded push-up)*
- 10 Pull-Up *(Scale: Banded pull-up or ring row)*
- 20 Air Squat *(Scale: Reduce reps to 15)*

**Target:** Complete all 5 rounds in 8–12 minutes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (8 min)

- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip external rotation, glute stretch*
- Foam Roll — Quads — 60 seconds each leg — *Reduce post-squat muscle tension*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *Address anterior hip tightness from squatting*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
Back squat is placed first to exploit full CNS availability for heavy loading. The metcon follows with bodyweight movements only to avoid posterior chain interference while targeting the glycolytic system through 8–12 minute time domain. This session structure prevents concurrent training interference by separating strength stimulus from conditioning load.
*— Wilson et al 2012 - Concurrent Training Meta-Analysis*

**Movement Selection**  
Back squat develops foundational lower body strength and is a fundamental squat pattern per CrossFit's 10 physical skills (Strength). The metcon uses push-ups and pull-ups to balance horizontal and vertical push/pull patterns while air squats provide lower body endurance without excessive loading that would compromise recovery.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
Week 1 baseline loading at 70% 1RM for 5x5 establishes technique and moderate volume per linear periodization principles. Straight sets allow consistent execution quality and work capacity development at this intensity. Rest periods of 180 seconds ensure phosphocreatine recovery between sets for strength adaptation.
*— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-STR-002 — Deadlift Wave Loading + AMRAP Power
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** strength

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- box
- jump rope

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal mobility prep*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Hip and thoracic mobility*
- Glute Bridge — 15 reps — *Glute activation, posterior chain prep*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Good Morning — 10 reps — *Hinge pattern activation*
- Romanian Deadlift — 8 reps — *Hamstring loading prep, deadlift movement prep*
- Box Jump — 5 reps — *Power output prep, hip extension activation*

### 💪 Deadlift — Wave Loading (24 min)

- **Deadlift** — 6×3-3-3-3-3-3 @ 75%-80%-85%-75%-82%-87% 1RM | 180-240 seconds between sets
  > *Wave loading: oscillate between heavier and lighter loads to exploit post-activation potentiation. First wave: 75-80-85%. Second wave: 77-82-87%. Heavy sets prime the nervous system for subsequent performance.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 12 minutes (14 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 10 Box Jump @ 24/20 inch box *(Scale: Step-up or reduce height)*
- 15 Kettlebell Swing (Russian) @ 53/35 lbs *(Scale: Reduce weight)*
- 30 Double-Under *(Scale: 90 single-unders)*

**Target:** 7–9 rounds

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*
- Foam Roll — Lats — 60 seconds each side — *Upper back tension release from deadlifting*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *Hip flexor lengthening post-hinge work*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
Deadlift targets the hinge pattern and posterior chain strength. The metcon follows with power-based movements (box jumps) that exploit residual neural activation from heavy deadlifts, paired with glycolytic conditioning. AMRAP format at 12 minutes targets the glycolytic-aerobic transition zone without excessive fatigue.
*— Gastin 2001 - Energy System Interaction*

**Movement Selection**  
Deadlift develops maximal strength in the hinge pattern. Box jumps paired post-deadlift utilize post-activation potentiation principles. KB swings reinforce the hinge pattern under fatigue, and double-unders add monostructural cardio per weekly balance requirements.
*— Robbins 2005 - Post-Activation Potentiation*

**Loading Rationale**  
Wave loading exploits PAP by oscillating between heavy and moderate loads. First wave (75-80-85%) primes the nervous system; second wave (77-82-87%) allows higher performance at similar percentages due to neural priming. This is appropriate for Week 2 strength emphasis with experienced athletes.
*— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training*

## WOD-STR-003 — Strict Press EMOM + Short Metcon
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** strength

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- rowing machine

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction — *Shoulder capsule mobility, controlled articular rotations*
- Banded Pull-Apart — 20 reps — *Scapular retraction, posterior shoulder activation*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Thoracic mobility for overhead position*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Pass-Through — 10 reps — *Shoulder mobility prep for overhead pressing*
- Strict Press — 8 reps — *Movement-specific warmup, groove pressing pattern*
- Push-Up — 10 reps — *Upper body activation, scapular stabilization*

### 💪 Strict Press — EMOM Strength (20 min)

- **Strict Press** — 10×3 @ 75% 1RM | E2MOM (Every 2 Minutes)
  > *E2MOM x 10 rounds = 20 minutes. Perform 3 reps at the top of each 2-minute interval. Faster execution earns more rest. Interval provides adequate phosphocreatine recovery for 75% loading.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 16 minutes (4 rounds) (18 min)

**Format:** EMOM | **Time cap:** 16 min

- 250/200 meters Row
- 12 Push Press @ 75/55 lbs *(Scale: Reduce load or reps to 9)*
- 10 Burpee *(Scale: Reduce to 8 reps or step-down burpee)*
- active recovery Rest

**Target:** Complete all work with 10–15 seconds rest each minute

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Reduce thoracic stiffness from pressing*
- Pigeon Pose — 60 seconds each side — *Hip mobility maintenance*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 slow reps each direction — *Post-pressing shoulder maintenance*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
Strict press develops vertical push strength under full CNS availability. EMOM format maintains session flow and auto-regulates rest. The metcon uses EMOM format with built-in rest to manage glycolytic fatigue across a 16-minute time domain, mixing monostructural cardio with pressing volume.
*— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement Selection**  
Strict press is the foundation for all vertical pressing per CrossFit's strength skill development. Push press in the metcon reinforces the pressing pattern under fatigue. Row provides monostructural cardio per weekly balance requirements, and burpees add full-body conditioning.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
E2MOM format at 75% 1RM for 3 reps per round provides adequate recovery (120 seconds) while maintaining engagement. This is appropriate for Week 1-2 baseline building with moderate intensity. The interval ensures phosphocreatine recovery without excessive downtime, training pacing discipline.
*— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-STR-004 — Front Squat Build + Aerobic Intervals
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** strength

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- assault bike

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Hip capsule prep*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Hip and thoracic mobility*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Thoracic extension for front rack position*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Squat pattern activation*
- Front Squat — 5-5-3-3 — *Progressive warmup to working weight*
- Inchworm — 8 reps — *Hamstring and shoulder prep*

### 💪 Front Squat — Ramp to Heavy (22 min)

- **Front Squat** — 4×5-4-3-2 @ 65%-72%-78%-83% 1RM | 180-240 seconds between sets
  > *Ramp/build-up: progressively increase load while decreasing reps. Prepares nervous system for heavier loading. Focus on upright torso and elbow position throughout.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 15 minutes (5 rounds) (16 min)

**Format:** EMOM | **Time cap:** 15 min

- 15/12 calories Assault Bike
- 12 Goblet Squat @ 53/35 lbs *(Scale: Reduce weight or reps to 10)*
- active recovery Rest

**Target:** Maintain consistent work across all 5 rounds

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*
- Foam Roll — Quads — 60 seconds each leg — *Reduce quad tension from squatting*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Restore thoracic mobility*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
Front squat requires thoracic extension and anterior core strength, developing squat pattern variation. Ramp loading prepares the nervous system progressively for heavier weights. The metcon uses aerobic intervals with built-in rest to develop oxidative capacity without interfering with strength recovery.
*— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement Selection**  
Front squat complements back squat by emphasizing upright torso and quad dominance. Assault bike provides low-impact monostructural cardio. Goblet squats reinforce the squat pattern under fatigue while maintaining front-loaded position similar to the strength work.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
Ramp/build-up progression (5@65%, 4@72%, 3@78%, 2@83%) is appropriate for Week 1 to develop technique under progressively challenging loads. This scheme reduces injury risk and allows motor pattern refinement, essential for the technically demanding front squat position.
*— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training*

## WOD-STR-005 — Bench Press Top Set + High-Rep Conditioning
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** strength

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- bench
- pull-up bar
- jump rope

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction — *Shoulder capsule mobility*
- Banded Pull-Apart — 20 reps — *Scapular activation, posterior shoulder warmup*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Thoracic mobility*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Push-Up — 15 reps — *Horizontal push activation*
- Bench Press — 10-8-5 — *Progressive warmup to working weight*
- Ring Row — 10 reps — *Antagonist activation, scapular control*

### 💪 Bench Press — Top Set + Volume (24 min)

- **Bench Press** — 5×2-5-5-5-5 @ 87% 1RM (set 1), 70% 1RM (sets 2-5) | 240 seconds after top set, 90 seconds between volume sets
  > *Top set + back-off volume: 1 heavy double (87%) for neural stimulation, followed by 4x5 at 70% for hypertrophy volume. Combines strength and structural adaptation efficiently.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 21-15-9 For Time (14 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 21-15-9 Pull-Up *(Scale: Banded pull-up or ring row)*
- 21-15-9 Push-Up *(Scale: Knee push-up)*
- 42-30-18 Single-Under *(Scale: Reduce reps by half)*

**Target:** Complete in 6–10 minutes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Foam Roll — Lats — 60 seconds each side — *Release lat tension from pulling and pressing*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Restore thoracic mobility post-bench*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 slow reps each direction — *Shoulder maintenance and recovery*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
Bench press develops horizontal push strength. Top set + back-off volume combines neural stimulation with hypertrophy work efficiently. The metcon balances push and pull (pull-ups and push-ups) per movement balance requirements while staying in the 6–10 minute glycolytic time domain.
*— Wilson et al 2012 - Concurrent Training Meta-Analysis*

**Movement Selection**  
Bench press addresses horizontal push per weekly movement balance. Pull-ups in the metcon ensure 1:1 push-pull ratio. Single-unders provide monostructural cardio. This combination develops both horizontal pushing strength and pulling endurance.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
Top set + back-off volume (1x2@87%, 4x5@70%) is appropriate for Week 3 peak intensity. The heavy double provides maximal neural stimulus while fresh, then volume sets at 70% accumulate structural adaptation. This scheme is time-efficient and suitable for intermediate athletes.
*— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

- Hang Power Clean — 8 reps — *Movement-specific warmup, groove pulling mechanics*
- Front Squat — 5 reps — *Rack position prep, receiving position*

### 💪 Power Clean — Technique EMOM (24 min)

- **Power Clean** — 8×2 @ 75% 1RM | E3MOM (Every 3 Minutes)
  > *E3MOM x 8 rounds = 24 minutes. Olympic lifting requires full CNS recovery between sets. 3-minute intervals ensure phosphocreatine replenishment and technique quality. Focus on explosive hip extension and fast elbows.*

**Format:** EMOM | **Time cap:** 14 min

- Foam Roll — Lats — 60 seconds each side — *Upper back release*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
Power clean develops explosive strength and power (rate of force development). E3MOM format ensures full recovery for Olympic lifting technique quality. Sprint intervals on ski erg develop phosphocreatine system power output without lower body impact that would compromise Olympic lift recovery.
*— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement Selection**  
Power clean is foundational for Olympic lifting skill development per CrossFit methodology. Ski erg provides upper body monostructural work that doesn't interfere with lower body recovery from cleans. This pairing develops power across both upper and lower body systems.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
E3MOM at 75% 1RM for doubles provides adequate recovery (180 seconds) for Olympic lifting technique quality. This is appropriate for Week 2 skill development. The 3-minute interval ensures phosphocreatine recovery and prevents neural fatigue that degrades complex motor patterns.
*— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-STR-007 — Overhead Squat Progression + Gymnastic Volume
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** strength

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- pull-up bar
- rings

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction — *Shoulder mobility for overhead position*
- Pass-Through — 10 reps — *Shoulder flexion and extension prep*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Hip and thoracic mobility*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Squat pattern activation*
- Overhead Squat — 10-8-5 — *Progressive warmup, establish overhead position*
- Hollow Body Hold — 2 x 20 seconds — *Midline activation for gymnastics prep*

### 💪 Overhead Squat — Ramp Build (22 min)

- **Overhead Squat** — 4×5-4-3-2 @ 60%-67%-73%-78% 1RM | 180 seconds between sets
  > *Ramp/build-up: progressively increase load while decreasing reps. OHS is technically complex and requires careful neural preparation. Focus on active shoulders, midline stability, and upright torso.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 14 minutes (16 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 14 min

- 7 Chest-to-Bar Pull-Up *(Scale: Pull-up or banded pull-up)*
- 7 Ring Dip *(Scale: Box dip or parallel bar dip)*
- 14 Burpee *(Scale: Reduce to 10 reps)*

**Target:** 8–11 rounds

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Restore thoracic mobility post-OHS*
- Foam Roll — Lats — 60 seconds each side — *Release lat tension from overhead and pulling work*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
Overhead squat develops the most technically demanding squat variation, requiring shoulder mobility, midline stability, and balance. Ramp loading prepares the CNS without excessive fatigue. The metcon uses gymnastics volume (pull-ups and dips) to satisfy weekly pull pattern requirements while developing upper body endurance.
*— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement Selection**  
OHS addresses squat pattern, shoulder stability, and coordination per CrossFit's 10 physical skills. Chest-to-bar and ring dips provide vertical push-pull balance. Burpees add full-body conditioning. This combination develops coordination, balance, and accuracy alongside strength.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
Ramp/build-up (5@60%, 4@67%, 3@73%, 2@78%) is appropriate for the complex OHS movement. Progressive loading allows technique refinement under increasing load, essential for overhead position stability. This scheme reduces injury risk from overhead loading and prepares the shoulder girdle systematically.
*— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training*
