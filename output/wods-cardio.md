# WOD Pool

## WOD-CARDIO-001 — Aerobic Capacity Builder
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** cardio

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- rowing machine
- assault bike
- pull-up bar

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Dynamic hip mobility drill*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Controlled articular rotations for shoulder health*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal mobility drill*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Full-body dynamic mobility sequence*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Easy Run — 400m — *Conversational pace to elevate heart rate*
- Air Squat — 10 reps — *Full depth, controlled tempo*
- Inchworm — 5 reps — *Hamstring and shoulder warmup*
- Row — 200m easy pace — *Activate posterior chain and cardiovascular system*

### 💪 Aerobic Power Development (22 min)

- **Row Intervals** — 6×500m @ moderate pace (70-75% max effort) | 90 seconds between intervals
  > *Target consistent split times. Focus on powerful leg drive and full recovery between efforts.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 20 minutes (20 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 20 min

- 15 calories Assault Bike @ bodyweight
- 200m Run @ bodyweight
- 10 reps Pull-Up @ bodyweight *(Scale: banded pull-up or ring row)*

**Target:** Complete as many rounds as possible. Target 6-8 rounds for trained athletes.

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (8 min)

- Easy Walk/Jog — 3 minutes — *Bring heart rate down gradually*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip opener stretch, breathe deeply*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *90/90 position, focus on anterior hip opening*
- Foam Roll - Quads — 90 seconds — *Slow passes, pause on tender areas*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
This session targets the oxidative energy system through sustained aerobic intervals and a long AMRAP. The rowing intervals develop aerobic power at sub-maximal intensity, while the 20-minute AMRAP maintains heart rate in the aerobic zone (65-75% max HR) to improve cardiovascular endurance. Gastin (2001) identifies the oxidative system as dominant beyond 3-4 minutes of effort, making this time domain ideal for aerobic base development.
*— Gastin 2001 - Energy System Interaction*

**Movement Selection**  
Monostructural movements (row, run, bike) are selected to minimize technical failure and keep intensity in the aerobic zone. Pull-ups are included in small sets to maintain work capacity without creating excessive muscular fatigue. This mixed-modal approach develops general physical preparedness across Glassman's cardiovascular endurance and stamina domains while preventing the monotony of single-modality cardio.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
The 500m row intervals at 70-75% effort allow sufficient recovery (90 seconds) to repeat quality efforts without entering the glycolytic system. The 20-minute AMRAP is designed for sustainable pacing - athletes should never reach failure on any movement. This polarized approach (80% of volume at low intensity) aligns with concurrent training principles to avoid interference with strength adaptations.
*— Wilson et al 2012 - Concurrent Training Meta-Analysis*

## WOD-CARDIO-002 — Glycolytic Threshold Work
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** cardio

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- assault bike

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Improve thoracic spine mobility for overhead position*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *Open anterior hip for better squat mechanics*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Shoulder joint preparation*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal warmup*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Assault Bike — 2 minutes easy — *Gradual heart rate elevation*
- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Full range of motion*
- Good Morning — 10 reps with empty barbell — *Hinge pattern activation*
- Push Press — 10 reps with empty barbell — *Movement-specific preparation*

### 💪 E2MOM Thruster Build (24 min)

- **Thruster (Front Squat + Push Press)** — 10×3 reps @ 70% 1RM Front Squat (or RPE 7-8) | Remainder of 2 minutes
  > *Build to a moderate load that can be moved with speed. Focus on explosive hip drive from bottom of squat into overhead press. This is technique work at moderate intensity, not max effort.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 5 rounds for time (15 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 15 min

- 15 reps Thruster @ 95/65 lbs (or 50% of strength block load)
- 12 calories Assault Bike @ bodyweight

**Target:** Target time: 10-13 minutes. This should be sustainable high effort, not all-out sprint.

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (8 min)

- Assault Bike Easy — 3 minutes — *Very low effort, active recovery pace*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Deep hip opener*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Release thoracic tension from overhead work*
- Foam Roll - Quads — 90 seconds — *Address quad fatigue from thrusters*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
This session targets the glycolytic energy system through repeated high-intensity efforts in the 10-15 minute time domain. The thruster-bike couplet creates sustained muscular and cardiovascular demand, training the body's ability to clear lactate and maintain power output under fatigue. The medium time domain (7-15 min) ensures glycolytic system dominance while avoiding excessive volume that would interfere with strength adaptation.
*— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement Selection**  
Thrusters combine squat and vertical push patterns, creating high metabolic demand through large muscle recruitment. The assault bike provides additional cardiovascular stress without technical complexity. This pairing develops stamina - the ability to process, deliver, store, and use energy - across Glassman's ten physical skills framework.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
The E2MOM strength format at 70% 1RM builds technique and speed at moderate loads, exploiting the 2-minute interval for phosphocreatine recovery between sets. The metcon uses 50% of this load to allow sustainable effort across 5 rounds without technical breakdown. The 1:3 work:rest ratio within each round (thruster sets + bike) aligns with glycolytic system recovery demands.
*— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-CARDIO-003 — Mixed-Modal Endurance
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** cardio

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- rowing machine
- kettlebell
- pull-up bar

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Hip joint preparation*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Shoulder mobility for pulling movements*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Full-body dynamic preparation*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Thoracic mobility for rowing mechanics*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Row — 500m easy — *Establish rowing mechanics at low intensity*
- Kettlebell Swing (Russian) — 10 reps light — *Hip hinge activation*
- Ring Row — 10 reps — *Scapular activation for pull-ups*
- Air Squat — 10 reps — *Lower body warmup*

### 💪 Kettlebell Swing Volume (22 min)

- **Kettlebell Swing (Russian)** — 5×20 reps @ 24/16 kg (moderate) | 90 seconds
  > *Focus on powerful hip extension, swing to eye level. This builds posterior chain endurance and power output. Maintain crisp hip snap on every rep.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 18 minutes (18 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 18 min

- 250m Row @ bodyweight
- 15 reps Kettlebell Swing (American) @ 24/16 kg
- 10 reps Pull-Up @ bodyweight *(Scale: banded pull-up or ring row)*

**Target:** Target 7-9 rounds. Pace should be consistent across all rounds - this is aerobic capacity work, not max effort sprinting.

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (8 min)

- Row Easy — 3 minutes — *Recovery pace, focus on breathing*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip mobility and recovery*
- Foam Roll - Lats — 90 seconds each side — *Address pulling muscle tightness*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Restore thoracic mobility*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
This session develops oxidative capacity through an 18-minute mixed-modal AMRAP that maintains heart rate in the aerobic zone. The combination of rowing, kettlebell swings, and pull-ups creates sustained cardiovascular demand while distributing muscular load across multiple movement patterns. The time domain (15-20 min) places primary stress on the oxidative energy system as identified by Gastin (2001).
*— Gastin 2001 - Energy System Interaction*

**Movement Selection**  
The movement selection balances monostructural cardio (row) with functional weightlifting (KB swing) and gymnastics (pull-up), embodying CrossFit's mixed-modal approach to GPP. The posterior chain emphasis (rowing, swings) develops the hinge pattern under sustained volume, while pull-ups maintain pulling strength capacity. This variety prevents local muscular failure while sustaining cardiovascular stress.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
The strength block uses straight sets at moderate load (5x20) to build kettlebell swing volume and work capacity. The metcon uses the same load but with American swings (overhead) and lower reps per round to manage fatigue across the 18-minute time domain. This concurrent approach - volume work followed by sustained conditioning - minimizes interference between strength and endurance adaptations when kept at moderate intensity.
*— Wilson et al 2012 - Concurrent Training Meta-Analysis*

## WOD-CARDIO-004 — Interval Power Development
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** cardio

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- ski erg
- box
- jump rope

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Shoulder preparation for ski erg pulling*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal mobility*
- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Hip joint warmup for jumping*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Dynamic full-body preparation*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Ski Erg — 2 minutes easy — *Establish pulling mechanics at low intensity*
- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Lower body activation*
- Box Jump (low box) — 5 reps — *Plyometric primer, focus on soft landing*
- Jump Rope — 50 single-unders — *Ankle and calf activation*

### 💪 Ski Erg Intervals (20 min)

- **Ski Erg Intervals** — 8×200m @ high effort (85-90% max) | 90 seconds
  > *Each interval should be near-maximal effort. Focus on powerful arm pull and full hip hinge. This develops anaerobic capacity and upper body pulling power.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 18 minutes (6 rounds of 3-movement sequence) (18 min)

**Format:** EMOM | **Time cap:** 18 min

- 10 reps Box Jump @ 24/20 inch box
- 30 reps Double-Under @ bodyweight *(Scale: 90 single-unders)*
- 12 calories Ski Erg @ bodyweight

**Target:** Complete all reps within each minute. Rest is the remainder of the minute. Target 15-30 seconds rest per movement.

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (10 min)

- Ski Erg Easy — 3 minutes — *Very low effort, active recovery*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip mobility after jumping volume*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *Address hip flexor tightness*
- Foam Roll - Lats — 90 seconds each side — *Release lat tension from ski erg*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Restore shoulder range of motion*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
This session targets the glycolytic energy system through high-intensity intervals in the strength block and sustained power output in the EMOM metcon. The ski erg intervals (200m at 85-90% effort) create repeated bouts of anaerobic work with incomplete recovery, training lactate clearance capacity. The EMOM format maintains elevated heart rate while allowing brief recovery between movements, sustaining effort in the 10-20 minute glycolytic-dominant time domain.
*— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement Selection**  
The movement selection emphasizes power development across multiple modalities. Box jumps develop lower body explosive power, double-unders train speed and coordination under fatigue, and ski erg intervals build upper body pulling power and cardiovascular capacity. This combination addresses Glassman's power (maximum force in minimum time) and speed (minimizing cycle time) physical skills while maintaining cardiovascular stress.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
The 200m ski erg intervals with 90-second rest create a 1:1 to 1:2 work:rest ratio appropriate for glycolytic system training. The EMOM metcon uses 60-second intervals to auto-regulate intensity - athletes must complete work efficiently to earn rest. This format prevents sandbagging while ensuring adequate recovery to maintain power output across all 18 minutes. The progression from high-intensity intervals to sustained EMOM work follows sound session sequencing.
*— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-CARDIO-005 — Engine Builder
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** cardio

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- assault bike
- rowing machine
- barbell
- plates

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Hip mobility preparation*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Shoulder joint preparation*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal mobility*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Dynamic full-body warmup*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Assault Bike — 2 minutes easy — *Gradual cardiovascular elevation*
- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Squat pattern warmup*
- Good Morning — 10 reps with empty barbell — *Hinge pattern activation*
- Sumo Deadlift High Pull — 8 reps with empty barbell — *Movement-specific preparation*

### 💪 Ramp to Moderate SDHP (22 min)

- **Sumo Deadlift High Pull** — 5×Sets of 8-8-8-6-6 @ Build from 45 lbs to 95/65 lbs across sets | 90 seconds between sets
  > *Ramp/buildup progression scheme. Start light to establish mechanics: wide stance, vertical torso, elbows high. Build to a moderate working weight that allows crisp hip extension and high pull on every rep. This is not max effort - focus on speed and technique.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 4 rounds for time (16 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 16 min

- 20 calories Assault Bike @ bodyweight
- 20 calories Row @ bodyweight
- 15 reps Sumo Deadlift High Pull @ 75/55 lbs

**Target:** Target time: 12-15 minutes. This should be sustained high effort with brief transitions between movements.

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (10 min)

- Row Easy — 3 minutes — *Very low effort, recovery pace*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip mobility and recovery*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *Release hip flexors from cycling volume*
- Foam Roll - Quads — 90 seconds — *Address quad fatigue*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Restore thoracic mobility*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
This session develops mixed oxidative-glycolytic capacity through sustained monostructural work and moderate-load SDHP. The 12-16 minute For Time format creates continuous cardiovascular demand while the movement rotation prevents local muscular failure. The combination of bike and row cardio with the hinge+pull pattern (SDHP) trains the body's ability to sustain power output across multiple energy pathways, characteristic of the medium time domain (7-15 min).
*— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement Selection**  
The monostructural cardio pieces (bike, row) provide high cardiovascular stress without technical complexity, allowing athletes to focus on output rather than movement quality. SDHP adds a functional weightlifting component that develops the posterior chain under fatigue. This mixed-modal approach embodies CrossFit GPP principles while keeping technical demand manageable under cardiovascular stress.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
The ramp/buildup progression scheme in the strength block (8-8-8-6-6 building load) provides neural preparation and technical refinement before the metcon. Starting light allows perfect mechanics; building across sets prepares the pattern under increasing load. Zatsiorsky & Kraemer identify progressive warm-up sets as essential for neural preparation and technique refinement. The metcon uses lighter load (75/55 vs 95/65) to maintain movement speed and prevent technical breakdown across 4 rounds.
*— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training*

## WOD-CARDIO-006 — Aerobic Base Development
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** cardio

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- rowing machine
- jump rope
- pull-up bar

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Shoulder preparation for rowing and pull-ups*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal mobility*
- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Hip joint warmup*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Dynamic full-body preparation*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Row — 500m easy — *Establish rowing mechanics*
- Jump Rope — 100 single-unders — *Ankle and calf activation*
- Ring Row — 10 reps — *Scapular activation*
- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Lower body warmup*

### 💪 Aerobic Row Intervals (20 min)

- **Row Intervals** — 5×3 minutes @ moderate pace (2:00-2:10/500m split, conversational effort) | 2 minutes between intervals
  > *Maintain consistent split time across all 5 intervals. This is aerobic base work - you should be able to hold a conversation. Focus on efficient stroke mechanics and breathing rhythm.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 18 minutes (18 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 18 min

- 15 calories Row @ bodyweight
- 60 single-unders Jump Rope @ bodyweight
- 8 reps Pull-Up @ bodyweight *(Scale: banded pull-up or ring row)*

**Target:** Target 8-10 rounds. Pace should be very consistent - this is long aerobic work, not sprint intervals.

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (10 min)

- Row Easy — 3 minutes — *Very low effort, focus on deep breathing*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Hip mobility*
- Foam Roll - Lats — 90 seconds each side — *Release pulling muscle tension*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Restore thoracic mobility*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Shoulder range of motion restoration*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
This session prioritizes aerobic base development through sustained low-to-moderate intensity work. The 3-minute row intervals with 2-minute rest build aerobic capacity without entering the glycolytic system, while the 18-minute AMRAP maintains heart rate in the aerobic zone (65-75% max HR). This session represents the ~80% low-intensity volume recommended in polarized training models to support concurrent strength and endurance adaptation.
*— Wilson et al 2012 - Concurrent Training Meta-Analysis*

**Movement Selection**  
Rowing provides low-impact cardiovascular stress with full-body engagement. Single-unders add rhythmic coordination work without high impact, and pull-ups maintain pulling strength in small manageable sets. This combination develops cardiovascular endurance (oxygen delivery capacity) and stamina (energy processing and utilization) across Glassman's ten physical skills while avoiding excessive muscular fatigue.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
The 3-minute intervals with 2-minute rest provide a 3:2 work:rest ratio appropriate for aerobic development - sufficient recovery to maintain quality without full phosphocreatine restoration. The conversational pace prescription ensures athletes stay below anaerobic threshold. The AMRAP uses moderate volume per movement (15 cal, 60 singles, 8 pull-ups) to prevent local failure and maintain sustainable aerobic output for the full 18 minutes, aligning with oxidative system dominance beyond 10+ minutes of continuous work.
*— Gastin 2001 - Energy System Interaction*

## WOD-CARDIO-007 — Lactate Threshold Challenge
**Duration:** 60 min | **Category:** cardio

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- assault bike
- barbell
- plates

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Hip mobility for cycling and squatting*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each arm — *Shoulder preparation*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal mobility*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Dynamic full-body warmup*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Assault Bike — 2 minutes easy — *Cardiovascular warmup*
- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Squat pattern activation*
- Good Morning — 10 reps with empty barbell — *Hinge pattern warmup*
- Front Squat — 8 reps with empty barbell — *Movement-specific preparation*

### 💪 E3MOM Front Squat Strength (24 min)

- **Front Squat** — 6×3 reps @ 80% 1RM (or RPE 8) | Remainder of 3 minutes
  > *Heavy loading requires full recovery between sets. E3MOM format provides adequate phosphocreatine restoration. Focus on upright torso, elbows high, full depth. This is strength work - move with control and precision.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 21-15-9 for time (14 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 14 min

- 21/15/9 calories Assault Bike @ bodyweight
- 21/15/9 reps Front Squat @ 95/65 lbs

**Target:** Target time: 8-12 minutes. This is a glycolytic burn - expect high heart rate and leg fatigue. Pace the bike to leave gas for the squats.

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (10 min)

- Assault Bike Easy — 3 minutes — *Very low effort, active recovery to clear lactate*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 seconds each side — *Deep hip stretch*
- Hip Flexor Stretch — 60 seconds each side — *Release hip flexors from squat and bike volume*
- Foam Roll - Quads — 2 minutes — *Address quad fatigue, slow deliberate passes*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Restore thoracic mobility*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session Design**  
This session targets lactate threshold through a descending-rep scheme (21-15-9) that maintains high intensity across 8-12 minutes. The bike-squat couplet creates continuous lower body demand with incomplete recovery between sets, training the body's ability to buffer hydrogen ions and maintain power output under accumulating fatigue. The medium time domain ensures glycolytic system dominance while the descending reps create psychological momentum as fatigue increases.
*— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

**Movement Selection**  
Front squats and assault bike both heavily tax the lower body, creating metabolic stress in the quadriceps and hip flexors. This intentional movement redundancy drives lactate accumulation and tests the athlete's ability to sustain power output when local muscular fatigue is high. The combination develops stamina (energy processing under fatigue) while maintaining the squat movement pattern under cardiovascular stress.
*— Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading Rationale**  
The E3MOM format in the strength block provides full phosphocreatine recovery for heavy front squats at 80% 1RM, allowing quality neural adaptation without systemic fatigue. The metcon uses light load (95/65 lbs, approximately 40-50% of strength load) to allow fast cycling and prevent technical breakdown under fatigue. The descending rep scheme (21-15-9) manages volume while maintaining intensity - total of 45 reps per movement keeps time domain in the glycolytic window (8-12 min target).
*— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*
