# WODs — Upper Body

## WOD-UB-001 — Press Power Foundation
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- pull-up bar

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 10 each direction — *Controlled articular rotations - slow, deliberate shoulder circles through full ROM*
- Banded Pull-Apart — 20 reps — *Light band, focus on scapular retraction*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Thoracic spine mobility*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Inchworm — 5 reps — *Walk hands out to plank, walk feet to hands*
- Push-Up — 10 reps — *Controlled tempo, full range*
- Empty Bar Strict Press — 2 x 5 reps — *Movement-specific prep, focus on bar path*

### 💪 Strict Press — Ramp to Heavy Double (22 min)

- **Strict Press** — 6×5, 4, 3, 2, 2, 2
  > *Progressive loading scheme. Bar path must be vertical. If 1RM unknown, build to RPE 9 on final double.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 12 minutes (15 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 8 Push Press *(Scale: Reduce load to maintain unbroken sets)*
- 12 Pull-Up *(Scale: Banded pull-up or ring row)*
- 16 Burpee *(Scale: Step-back burpee, omit jump)*

**Target:** 8-10+ rounds for experienced athletes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (8 min)

- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Hip flexor, thoracic rotation, hamstring stretch combo*
- Foam Roll — Lats — 90 sec each side — *Target latissimus from armpit to mid-back*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction — *Slow, controlled, end-range emphasis*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** Vertical pressing strength is placed first to exploit full CNS availability. The metcon uses moderate-load pressing paired with pulling to maintain movement balance (push:pull ratio 1:1.5 favoring pull) and targets the glycolytic system with 12-minute AMRAP duration. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed, Ch. 17; Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Movement selection:** Strict press is the foundation for all overhead strength. Ramp loading allows progressive neural preparation under increasing load. Push press in metcon uses previously fatigued pressing musculature at moderate intensity, training strength-endurance. Pull-ups provide antagonist balance. *— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training, Ch. 3; CrossFit Level 2 Training Guide*

**Loading rationale:** Ramp/buildup scheme (5→4→3→2 reps at 65%→85%) prepares the nervous system for heavier loads while developing technique under progressively challenging weights. This is appropriate for Week 1 baseline establishment or complex movement introduction. Metcon load kept moderate (95/65 lb) to allow volume accumulation without technical breakdown. *— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training; Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-UB-002 — Bench and Pull Density
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- bench
- pull-up bar

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Band Pass-Through — 15 reps — *Use resistance band, wide grip, open shoulders*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Quadruped position, hand behind head, rotate through mid-back*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Thoracic and lumbar mobility*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Push-Up — 3 x 5 reps — *Tempo: 3 sec down, 1 sec up*
- Ring Row — 3 x 8 reps — *Body angle to allow controlled reps*
- Empty Bar Bench Press — 2 x 8 reps — *Movement prep, touch chest, full lockout*

### 💪 Bench Press — E2MOM Build + Chest-to-Bar Skill (24 min)

- **Bench Press** — 8×4 reps per round
  > *Auto-regulated rest. If reps take 30 sec, you rest 90 sec. Build load across rounds: start 70%, finish 80%.*
- **Chest-to-Bar Pull-Up Skill Work** — 4×3-5 reps (quality focus)
  > *Focus on pulling chest to bar, not just chin over. If cannot perform strict, use band assistance. Scaling: strict pull-up to chest height.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 5 Rounds (14 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 15 Push-Up *(Scale: Banded push-up or hand-release push-up)*
- 10 Pull-Up *(Scale: Banded pull-up or ring row x 15)*
- 200m Row *(Scale: Reduce to 150m)*

**Target:** 8-11 minutes for experienced athletes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Foam Roll — Lats — 90 sec each side — *Armpit to mid-back*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 sec each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*
- Banded Pull-Apart — 2 x 20 reps — *Light band, scapular retraction focus*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** Horizontal pressing (bench press) is a foundational upper-body strength pattern. The EMOM format maintains session flow and rewards efficient execution. Chest-to-bar skill work develops vertical pulling proficiency. The metcon uses bodyweight push/pull movements plus monostructural cardio, targeting glycolytic and oxidative systems with 5-round structure (~10 min duration). *— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed; Gastin 2001 - Energy System Interaction*

**Movement selection:** Bench press and chest-to-bar pull-up together provide balanced horizontal push and vertical pull volume. Push:pull ratio is 1:1 in strength block (4 sets bench, 4 sets C2B) and maintained in metcon. This prevents shoulder imbalance and supports long-term joint health. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed; CrossFit Level 2 Training Guide*

**Loading rationale:** E2MOM format with progressive loading (70%→80%) builds work capacity at moderate-heavy intensity while maintaining pacing discipline. The 2-minute interval provides adequate phosphocreatine recovery for 4-rep sets at these intensities. Metcon kept to bodyweight movements to avoid excessive loading after heavy bench work. *— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed; Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*

## WOD-UB-003 — Ring Strength Progression
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- rings
- pull-up bar
- rowing machine

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 10 each direction — *Controlled articular rotations - prepare shoulder for ring instability*
- Banded Pull-Apart — 2 x 15 reps — *Posterior shoulder and scapular activation*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal mobility*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Push-Up — 10 reps — *Controlled tempo*
- Ring Row — 3 x 8 reps — *Body angle progression: start easy, finish challenging*
- Ring Support Hold — 3 x 20 sec — *Active shoulders, stable rings, hollow body tension*

### 💪 Ring Dip Strength + Hollow Body Foundation (22 min)

- **Ring Dip** — 5×5 reps
  > *If cannot perform 5 strict ring dips, scale to parallel bar dip or box dip. Maintain ring stability throughout.*
- **Hollow Body Hold** — 4×30 sec
  > *Lower back pressed to floor, arms overhead, legs straight and low. If form breaks, bend knees to maintain position.*
- **Arch Body Hold** — 4×30 sec
  > *Opposite of hollow - chest and thighs off ground, arms and legs extended. Alternate with hollow holds.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 12 min (4 movements x 3 rounds) (12 min)

**Format:** EMOM | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 8 Pull-Up *(Scale: Banded pull-up or ring row x 12)*
- 12 Push-Up *(Scale: Knee push-up or hand-release push-up)*
- 12/10 cal Row *(Scale: Reduce to 10/8 cal)*
- 10 Burpee *(Scale: Step-back burpee)*

**Target:** Complete all reps within each minute with 10-15 sec rest

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (8 min)

- Foam Roll — Lats — 90 sec each side — *Target latissimus and teres major*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction, slow — *End-range exploration, no pain*
- Pigeon Pose — 2 min each side — *Hip external rotation and hip flexor stretch*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** Ring dips require high shoulder stability and are foundational for ring muscle-up progression. Hollow and arch body holds develop the body tension prerequisite for all gymnastics movements. The EMOM metcon maintains intensity while allowing brief recovery each minute, targeting the glycolytic system with 12-minute duration. *— Sommer - Building the Gymnastic Body; CrossFit Gymnastics Specialty Course; Gastin 2001 - Energy System Interaction*

**Movement selection:** Ring dips develop vertical pushing strength under unstable conditions, requiring greater stabilizer recruitment than parallel bar dips. Hollow and arch holds are non-negotiable foundations per Sommer's gymnastics progression model. The metcon balances push (push-ups, burpees) and pull (pull-ups, row) at 1:1 ratio. *— Sommer - Building the Gymnastic Body; CrossFit Gymnastics Specialty Course*

**Loading rationale:** Straight sets at RPE 7-8 (5x5 ring dips) build baseline strength-endurance in the movement without excessive CNS fatigue. This loading is appropriate for Week 1-2 baseline phases or when introducing unstable surface work. Hollow/arch holds use fixed 30-sec duration to develop isometric endurance. *— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed; Sommer - Building the Gymnastic Body*

## WOD-UB-004 — Push Press Volume + Pulling Capacity
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- pull-up bar
- assault bike

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Band Pass-Through — 15 reps — *Wide grip, open shoulders and chest*
- Shoulder CARs — 8 each direction — *Slow, controlled, full range*
- Hip Circles — 10 each direction — *Leg extended, draw circles with foot*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Depth and hip extension focus*
- Empty Bar Push Press — 3 x 5 reps — *Dip, drive, press - establish bar path*
- Pull-Up or Ring Row — 2 x 5 reps — *Prime pulling musculature*

### 💪 Push Press — Top Set + Back-Off Volume (20 min)

- **Push Press** — 5×1 x 3 @ heavy, then 4 x 6 @ moderate
  > *Top set + back-off scheme: neural stimulation (heavy triple) followed by hypertrophy volume (4x6 at lighter load). If 1RM unknown, build to RPE 8-9 on triple, then reduce ~15-20% for back-off sets.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 4 Rounds (15 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 15 min

- 12 Chest-to-Bar Pull-Up *(Scale: Regular pull-up x 15 or banded C2B x 12)*
- 20 Push-Up *(Scale: Banded push-up or hand-release push-up)*
- 15/12 cal Assault Bike *(Scale: Reduce to 12/10 cal or row 200m)*

**Target:** 10-14 minutes for experienced athletes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (10 min)

- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Hip flexor, hamstring, thoracic rotation combo*
- Foam Roll — Lats — 2 min each side — *Armpit to mid-back, pause on tender spots*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction, very slow — *Post-workout end-range work*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 sec each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** Push press combines pressing strength with lower body drive, developing power and work capacity. The top set + back-off volume scheme allows peak intensity exposure without excessive CNS fatigue, followed by structural hypertrophy work. The metcon emphasizes vertical pulling capacity (C2B) balanced against horizontal pushing (push-ups) with monostructural cardio intervals. *— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed, Ch. 9; Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Movement selection:** Push press is a hybrid movement training the press pattern with explosive leg drive. C2B pull-ups develop vertical pulling strength beyond standard pull-ups. Push:pull ratio in metcon is 1:1 (20 push-ups : 12 C2B adjusted for difficulty). Assault bike provides glycolytic stress without additional upper-body loading. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed; CrossFit Level 2 Training Guide*

**Loading rationale:** Top set + back-off volume scheme (1x3@85%, 4x6@70%) exploits neural priming from heavy loading (post-activation potentiation) while accumulating hypertrophy volume at moderate intensity. This is efficient for concurrent training and appropriate for Week 3 peak intensity sessions. The heavy top set happens fresh; volume work follows with reduced load. *— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed, Ch. 9; Robbins 2005 - Post-Activation Potentiation*

## WOD-UB-005 — Barbell Row Foundation + HSPU Skill
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- wall
- rowing machine

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Quadruped, hand behind head, rotate through mid-back*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal articulation*
- Band Pass-Through — 15 reps — *Shoulder flexion and extension mobility*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Inchworm — 5 reps — *Walk out to plank, walk feet to hands*
- Good Morning (empty bar) — 10 reps — *Hip hinge pattern, posterior chain activation*
- Empty Bar Barbell Row — 2 x 8 reps — *Hip hinge position, pull to sternum*

### 💪 Barbell Row — Wave Loading + HSPU Progression (23 min)

- **Barbell Row (Bent Over)** — 6×5, 5, 5, 5, 5, 5
  > *Wave loading: oscillate between moderate and heavy loads. Maintain hip hinge position throughout. Pull to lower sternum, elbows back. If 1RM unknown, use RPE and build waves.*
- **Handstand Push-Up (or progression)** — 4×3-5 reps (quality focus)
  > *If cannot perform strict HSPU, scale to: deficit pike push-up, box pike push-up, or wall-facing HSPU with abmat. Focus on vertical torso and full lockout.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 10 minutes (13 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 10 min

- 10 Push-Up *(Scale: Banded or knee push-up)*
- 15 Ring Row *(Scale: Adjust body angle (more upright = easier))*
- 150m Row *(Scale: Reduce to 125m)*

**Target:** 7-9 rounds for experienced athletes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (9 min)

- Foam Roll — Lats — 90 sec each side — *Target latissimus and teres major*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Hip flexor, hamstring, T-spine rotation*
- Pigeon Pose — 2 min each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction — *Slow, end-range, pain-free*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** Barbell rows develop horizontal pulling strength, often undertrained relative to vertical pulling. HSPU skill work addresses vertical pressing in an inverted position. The metcon maintains push:pull balance (10 push-ups : 15 ring rows, adjusted for difficulty) and includes monostructural rowing to target oxidative system. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed; CrossFit Gymnastics Specialty Course*

**Movement selection:** Barbell rows target the posterior chain (lats, rhomboids, traps) with heavy loading potential. Wave loading exploits post-activation potentiation by alternating heavy and moderate loads. HSPU (or progressions) develop vertical pressing in a gymnastics context, requiring shoulder stability and core tension. *— Robbins 2005 - Post-Activation Potentiation; Sommer - Building the Gymnastic Body*

**Loading rationale:** Wave loading (72%→77%→82%→72%→80%→85%) allows volume accumulation at high intensity by oscillating load. Heavy sets prime the nervous system for subsequent performance. This scheme is appropriate for Week 2-3 strength emphasis with experienced athletes. The waves prevent linear fatigue accumulation while maintaining intensity. *— Robbins 2005 - Post-Activation Potentiation; Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed*

## WOD-UB-006 — Gymnastics Capacity + Upper Body Endurance
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- pull-up bar
- rings
- jump rope

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Shoulder CARs — 10 each direction — *Prepare shoulders for high-volume gymnastics*
- Banded Pull-Apart — 2 x 20 reps — *Posterior shoulder activation*
- Cat-Cow — 10 reps — *Spinal mobility*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Push-Up — 3 x 8 reps — *Controlled tempo, full range*
- Ring Row — 3 x 10 reps — *Progressive body angle*
- Pull-Up — 2 x 3 reps — *Strict, prime CNS*

### 💪 Hollow/Arch Foundation + Pull-Up Volume (22 min)

- **Hollow Body Hold** — 5×30 sec
  > *Lower back pressed to floor, arms overhead, legs straight and low. Non-negotiable gymnastics foundation. Scale: bend knees if form breaks.*
- **Arch Body Hold** — 5×30 sec
  > *Chest and thighs off ground, arms and legs extended. Build to 30 sec without compensation.*
- **Pull-Up (strict or kipping)** — 5×Max reps in 30 sec
  > *Work:rest ratio ~1:3. Goal is total volume, not singles. If strict, aim for 3-8 reps. If kipping, 8-15 reps per set. Scale: banded pull-up or ring row.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 12 minutes (14 min)

**Format:** AMRAP | **Time cap:** 12 min

- 8 Burpee *(Scale: Step-back burpee)*
- 12 Ring Row *(Scale: Adjust angle (more upright = easier))*
- 30 Double-Under *(Scale: Single-under x 90)*
- 10 Push-Up *(Scale: Banded or knee push-up)*

**Target:** 6-8 rounds for experienced athletes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (9 min)

- Foam Roll — Lats — 2 min each side — *Armpit to mid-back, pause on tender areas*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction, very slow — *End-range exploration, no pain*
- Pigeon Pose — 2 min each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 5 each side — *Full-body mobility sequence*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** Hollow and arch body holds are foundational for all gymnastics movements per Sommer's progression model. High-volume pull-up work (5x30sec max effort sets) develops muscular endurance and work capacity. The metcon uses bodyweight movements only, targeting muscular endurance and glycolytic system with 12-minute AMRAP. *— Sommer - Building the Gymnastic Body; CrossFit Gymnastics Specialty Course; Gastin 2001 - Energy System Interaction*

**Movement selection:** Hollow and arch holds are non-negotiable prerequisites for kipping, muscle-ups, and handstand work. Pull-up volume builds pulling capacity. Metcon maintains push:pull balance (10 push-ups + 8 burpees : 12 ring rows, ~1:1 ratio). Double-unders add monostructural conditioning without upper-body load. *— Sommer - Building the Gymnastic Body; Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Loading rationale:** Max rep sets in fixed time (30 sec work, 90 sec rest) create a 1:3 work:rest ratio appropriate for strength-endurance. This develops work capacity without compromising movement quality. Straight sets at bodyweight for hollow/arch holds build isometric endurance progressively (5x30 sec). *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed; Sommer - Building the Gymnastic Body*

## WOD-UB-007 — Overhead Strength Complex + Mixed Modal
**Duration:** 60 min

### 🎒 Equipment Needed

- barbell
- plates
- rack
- pull-up bar
- ski erg

### 🧘 Static Warmup (5 min)

- Band Pass-Through — 15 reps — *Wide grip, open shoulders*
- Shoulder CARs — 10 each direction — *Controlled articular rotations*
- Thoracic Rotation — 10 each side — *Quadruped, hand behind head*

### 🏃 Active Warmup (5 min)

- Air Squat — 15 reps — *Full depth, hip extension*
- Empty Bar Strict Press — 10 reps — *Vertical bar path*
- Empty Bar Push Jerk — 3 x 3 reps — *Dip-drive-catch pattern*

### 💪 Press + Push Jerk Complex — E3MOM Build (24 min)

- **Strict Press + Push Jerk Complex** — 7×2 Strict Press + 1 Push Jerk per round
  > *Perform 2 strict presses, then 1 push jerk without dropping the bar. Build load across rounds. E3MOM provides full PCr recovery for each complex. If 1RM unknown, start at RPE 6-7 and build to RPE 8.*

### 🔥 Metcon — 21-15-9 (14 min)

**Format:** For Time | **Time cap:** 14 min

- 21-15-9 Push Jerk *(Scale: Reduce to 75/55 lb or push press)*
- 21-15-9 Pull-Up *(Scale: Banded pull-up or ring row (add 6 reps: 27-21-15))*
- 21-15-9 cal Ski Erg *(Scale: Reduce to 18-12-6 cal or row same calories)*

**Target:** 8-12 minutes for experienced athletes

### 🧊 Cooldown & Mobility (7 min)

- Foam Roll — Lats — 90 sec each side — *Target latissimus and teres major*
- Shoulder CARs — 5 each direction, slow — *Post-workout shoulder mobility*
- Pigeon Pose — 90 sec each side — *Hip external rotation stretch*
- World's Greatest Stretch — 3 each side — *Full-body mobility sequence*

### 🔬 Why This Session?

**Session design:** Combining strict press with push jerk in a complex taxes the pressing musculature under different loading conditions (grinding strength vs. explosive power). The E3MOM format provides full phosphocreatine recovery (3 min) appropriate for heavy complexes. The 21-15-9 metcon structure creates a descending rep scheme targeting glycolytic system with ~10 min duration. *— Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed; Glassman 2002 - CrossFit Journal*

**Movement selection:** Strict press develops pure pressing strength; push jerk adds explosive power and leg drive. Pairing them without dropping the bar increases time under tension. Metcon maintains push:pull ratio (push jerk : pull-up 1:1) and includes ski erg for upper-body dominant monostructural work. *— Zatsiorsky & Kraemer - Science and Practice of Strength Training; CrossFit Level 2 Training Guide*

**Loading rationale:** E3MOM with progressive loading (65%→80%) allows gradual intensity increase while maintaining quality. The 3-minute interval is necessary for complex work requiring multiple reps per round. Building across 7 rounds accumulates volume (14 presses + 7 jerks) at increasing intensity. This is appropriate for Week 2 baseline building or complex movement introduction. *— Bompa & Haff - Periodization 5th ed; Haff & Triplett - NSCA Essentials 4th ed*
