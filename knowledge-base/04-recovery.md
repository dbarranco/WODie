# Recovery and Weekly Structure

**Source:** Haff & Triplett, *NSCA Essentials of Strength and Conditioning*, 4th ed., Ch. 21, 2016.
**Source:** Kreher, J.B. & Schwartz, J.B. "Overtraining Syndrome." *Sports Health* 4(2), 2012.

---

## Recovery Principles

Adaptation does not happen during training — it happens during recovery. Training is the stimulus; recovery is where the adaptation occurs. Insufficient recovery prevents adaptation and leads to overreaching or overtraining syndrome.

### Recovery Timelines by Training Stress

| Session Type | CNS Recovery | Muscular Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| Heavy lower body (>85% 1RM squat/deadlift) | 48–72h | 48–72h |
| Heavy upper body (>85% 1RM press/pull) | 24–48h | 24–48h |
| Olympic lifting (technical, moderate load) | 24–48h | 24h |
| High-intensity metcon (glycolytic, <15 min) | 24h | 24h |
| Aerobic work (moderate intensity, <30 min) | <24h | <24h |
| Skill work (gymnastics, low load) | <24h | <24h |

### Central Nervous System (CNS) Fatigue

Heavy loaded movements (>85% 1RM), maximal sprints, and complex Olympic lifting create CNS fatigue independently of muscular damage. CNS fatigue causes:
- Reduced power output
- Slower reaction time
- Degraded motor pattern quality

**Rule:** No two high-CNS-demand sessions back to back. Heavy lower body + Olympic lifting cannot appear on consecutive days.

## Weekly Structure Rules

These rules are hard constraints. The generator must respect all of them.

### Frequency Rules
- **Olympic lifting:** Maximum 3 sessions per week. Never on consecutive days.
- **Heavy lower body (squat/deadlift >80% 1RM):** Maximum 2 sessions per week.
- **Heavy upper body (press >80% 1RM):** Maximum 2 sessions per week.
- **High-intensity metcon:** Maximum 4 sessions per week.
- **Pure aerobic work:** Minimum 1 session per week.

### Sequencing Rules
- Never schedule heavy posterior chain (deadlift, Romanian DL, GHD) the day after heavy squats
- Never schedule Olympic lifting after a heavy lower body session
- Never schedule two "For Time" metcons on consecutive days
- Always have at least one lower-intensity or active recovery session per week

### Movement Balance (per week)
- Push:Pull ratio must be ≥ 1:1 (equal or more pulling than pushing)
- Both squat and hinge patterns must appear every week
- Both horizontal and vertical push/pull should appear each week
- At least one monostructural cardio piece (row, bike, run, ski) per week

---

## The Deload

A deload is a planned reduction in training stress, typically in week 4 of a 4-week cycle. It is not optional — it is where supercompensation (performance improvement above baseline) occurs.

**Deload parameters:**
- Volume reduced by 40–60% (fewer sets)
- Intensity reduced to 60–65% 1RM
- Metcon intensity reduced (aerobic pace, not maximal effort)
- No new skill introduction during deload

**Reference:** Bompa & Haff, *Periodization*, Ch. 6, 2009.

## Signs of Inadequate Recovery (for rationale text)

The following are evidence-based markers of overreaching:
- Persistent elevated resting heart rate (>7 bpm above baseline for >3 days)
- Decreased performance across multiple sessions
- Sleep disturbance, mood changes, appetite loss

These are not tracked by the app, but the rationale text should remind users that rest days are non-negotiable, not optional.
