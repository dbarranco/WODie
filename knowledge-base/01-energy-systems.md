## The Three Energy Systems

The body uses three energy systems simultaneously. Their relative contribution depends on exercise intensity and duration. Training must target each system appropriately and in the right sequence within a session.

### 1. Phosphocreatine System (ATP-PC)
- **Duration:** 0–10 seconds of maximal effort
- **Fuel:** Phosphocreatine stored in muscle
- **Recovery:** ~2–3 minutes for full replenishment
- **CrossFit application:** Heavy singles, max effort lifts, short sprints, box jumps
- **Training prescription:** Work intervals <10 sec, rest 2–3 min, fully recovered before next set

### 2. Glycolytic System (Anaerobic Glycolysis)
- **Duration:** 10 seconds to ~2 minutes of high-intensity effort
- **Fuel:** Muscle glycogen → lactate
- **Byproduct:** Hydrogen ions (cause muscular fatigue, not lactic acid as commonly misunderstood)
- **CrossFit application:** Moderate-length metcons, sprint intervals, cycling efforts
- **Training prescription:** Work 20 sec–2 min at high intensity, rest 1:2 to 1:3 work:rest ratio

### 3. Oxidative System (Aerobic)
- **Duration:** >2 minutes, dominant beyond 3–4 minutes
- **Fuel:** Glycogen, fat, protein (minimal)
- **CrossFit application:** Long metcons, aerobic base work, monostructural cardio
- **Training prescription:** Sustained effort >10 minutes for base development; polarized model keeps ~80% of aerobic volume at low intensity

---

## Energy System Targeting in Session Design

- **Session should not try to train all three systems equally** — this blunts adaptation
- Within a session: heavy strength work first (ATP-PC, full CNS availability), then short/medium metcon (glycolytic or mixed)
- Long oxidative sessions are standalone — do not follow heavy lifting
- Alternating energy system emphasis across days reduces interference between strength and endurance adaptations

**Reference:** Wilson et al. "Concurrent Training: A Meta-Analysis Examining Interference of Aerobic and Resistance Exercises." *Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research* 26(8), 2012.

## Practical Time Domain Guidelines for Metcons

| Time Domain | Primary System | Max Duration | Rest Before Next Session |
|---|---|---|---|
| Short | Phosphocreatine / early glycolytic | <7 min | 24h |
| Medium | Glycolytic / mixed | 7–15 min | 24–36h |
| Long | Oxidative | 15–25 min | 36–48h |

**Rule:** Sessions in this app use short and medium time domains only. Long time domain metcons (>20 min) are excluded from regular 60-minute sessions to preserve the strength adaptation and prevent excessive systemic fatigue.
