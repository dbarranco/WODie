# CrossFit Methodology and Physical Skills

**Source:** Glassman, G. "What is Fitness?" *CrossFit Journal*, October 2002.
**Source:** CrossFit Level 2 Training Guide, CrossFit Inc., 2022.

---

## The 10 Physical Skills

CrossFit defines fitness as competency across ten physical skills. A program is only as good as the number of skills it develops. Neglecting any skill creates a gap that limits total performance.

| Skill | Definition | Primary Adaptation |
|---|---|---|
| Cardiovascular endurance | Ability to gather, process, and deliver oxygen | Cardiorespiratory |
| Stamina | Ability to process, deliver, store, and use energy | Cardiorespiratory + muscular |
| Strength | Ability to apply force | Muscular |
| Flexibility | Ability to maximize range of motion at a joint | Connective tissue |
| Power | Ability to apply maximum force in minimum time | Muscular + neural |
| Speed | Ability to minimize cycle time of repeated movement | Neural |
| Coordination | Ability to combine several distinct movement patterns | Neural |
| Agility | Ability to minimize transition time between movements | Neural |
| Balance | Ability to control center of gravity relative to support base | Neural |
| Accuracy | Ability to control movement in a given direction at given intensity | Neural |

**Key principle:** Skills trained through practice (coordination, agility, balance, accuracy) cannot be developed through non-specific conditioning. They require deliberate skill work — this is the basis for the skill block in every session.

## General Physical Preparedness (GPP)

CrossFit targets **GPP** — broad, general, inclusive fitness — as opposed to specialized performance. The goal is to prepare for any physical challenge, not to optimize one narrow metric.

GPP is achieved through:
- **Constantly varied** functional movement
- Executed at **high intensity** (relative to individual capacity)
- Across **broad modal and time domains**

## Session Structure Rationale

The canonical CrossFit session follows this structure for physiological reasons:

**1. Warmup (general + specific)**
- General: elevates core temperature, increases synovial fluid in joints, raises heart rate
- Specific: prepares the exact movement patterns used in the session
- Static stretching before high-intensity work does not improve performance and may reduce power output temporarily — place static mobility work at the end
- **Rule:** Static warmup (mobility, joint prep) comes first; active warmup (movement-specific activation) second

**Reference:** Behm et al. "Acute Effects of Muscle Stretching on Physical Performance, Range of Motion, and Injury Incidence in Healthy Active Individuals." *Applied Physiology, Nutrition, and Metabolism*, 41(1), 2016.

**2. Skill/Strength Block**
- Placed before metcon to ensure CNS freshness and technique quality
- Heavy loading requires full neural availability — compromised by prior fatigue
- Skill development (gymnastics, Olympic lifting technique) degrades rapidly under fatigue

**3. Metcon**
- After strength work, glycogen is partially depleted — metcon intensity must account for this
- Time domains kept short to medium to avoid excessive catabolism
- Format (AMRAP, EMOM, For Time) chosen to match the energy system target for that session

**4. Cooldown and Mobility**
- Parasympathetic nervous system recovery begins
- Static stretching is appropriate here — muscles are warm, range gains are more durable post-exercise
- Foam rolling and soft tissue work reduce perceived soreness

## WOD Formats

| Format | Description | Primary Stimulus |
|---|---|---|
| AMRAP | As Many Rounds/Reps As Possible in fixed time | Aerobic power, pacing |
| EMOM | Every Minute On the Minute | Consistent output, recovery management |
| For Time | Complete fixed work as fast as possible | Maximum effort, anaerobic threshold |
| RFT | Rounds For Time | Volume at speed |
| Chipper | Long list of varied movements, done once through | Volume, mental endurance |

**Rule for this app:** Chippers are excluded from regular sessions (time cap incompatible with 60-minute total). AMRAPs capped at 15 minutes. For Time workouts must have a time cap ≤15 minutes.
